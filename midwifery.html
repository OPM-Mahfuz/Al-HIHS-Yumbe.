Midwifery Program

Program Description

The Midwifery program prepares students to provide quality maternal and newborn care, focusing on antenatal, delivery, and postnatal services. The program emphasizes practical skills, community engagement, and ethical practice to ensure safe deliveries and improved maternal health outcomes in underserved communities.

Curriculum Overview

  • Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Maternal and Child Health Nursing
  • Labor and Delivery Care
  • Postnatal Care and Family Planning
  • Community Midwifery and Outreach
  • Medical Ethics and Patient Rights
  • Practical Training and Internships

Entry Requirements

  • Ugandan Certificate of Education (UCE) with passes in English, Mathematics, Biology, and Chemistry
  • Minimum of 5 passes in O-Level
  • Relevant health-related certificate or diploma (preferred)
  • Age: 17 years and above
  • Interview and health screening
