Clinical Medicine Program

Program Description

The Clinical Medicine program prepares students to become medical practitioners capable of diagnosing, treating, and preventing illnesses. The curriculum combines rigorous theoretical coursework with practical clinical training to produce competent health professionals who can serve in diverse healthcare settings, especially in underserved regions.

Curriculum Overview

  • Basic Medical Sciences: Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry
  • Pathology and Microbiology
  • Pharmacology
  • Clinical Skills and Patient Care
  • Community and Public Health
  • Internship and Practical Rotations

Entry Requirements

  • Ugandan Certificate of Education (UCE) with passes in English, Biology, Chemistry, and Mathematics
  • Minimum of 5 passes in O-Level
  • Relevant sciences or pre-med diploma (preferred)
  • Age: 17 years and above
  • Interview and health assessment
